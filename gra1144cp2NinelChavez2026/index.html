Strawberry Cheesecake

This strawberry cheesecake is creamy, smooth, and topped with a sweet, slightly tangy strawberry sauce that makes every bite feel special. It’s one of those desserts that looks fancy but is pretty simple to make. Perfect for birthdays, family gatherings, or just when you’re craving something sweet and comforting. Don’t worry if you’re new to baking this recipe walks you through everything step by step.

Ingredients

Crust

  • 2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • ½ cup melted butter
  • 2 tablespoons sugar

Cheesecake Filling

  • 24 oz (3 blocks) cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup sour cream

Strawberry Topping

  • 2 cups fresh strawberries, sliced
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch + 2 tablespoons water

Steps/Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (163°C). Lightly grease a 9-inch springform pan.
  2. Make the crust: In a bowl, mix graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and sugar. Press the mixture firmly into the bottom of the pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then let it cool.
  3. Prepare the filling: In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth. Add sugar and vanilla, mixing until combined. Add eggs one at a time, mixing gently. Finally, fold in the sour cream.
  4. Bake the cheesecake: Pour the filling over the cooled crust. Bake for 50â€“60 minutes, until the center is slightly jiggly. Turn off the oven and let it cool inside with the door cracked open.
  5. Make the strawberry topping: In a saucepan, cook strawberries, sugar, and lemon juice over medium heat. Stir in the cornstarch mixture and cook until thickened. Let it cool.
  6. Assemble: Spread the strawberry topping over the chilled cheesecake. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours before serving.
